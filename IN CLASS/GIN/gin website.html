About Spider-Man

Spider-Man is a fictional superhero created by writer Stan Lee and writer-artist Steve Ditko. He first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in 1962. The character's secret identity is Peter Parker, a high school student living in New York City who gains superpowers after being bitten by a radioactive spider.

History

Spider-Man's origins have been told in various media, but the core story remains the same. After the death of his Uncle Ben, Peter learns that "with great power comes great responsibility," a lesson that drives him to use his powers to fight crime.

Powers & Abilities

Spider-Man possesses a variety of superhuman abilities, including wall-crawling, super strength, agility, and a "spider-sense" that warns him of danger. He is also an expert in hand-to-hand combat and a skilled scientist and inventor.

Media Appearances

Spider-Man has appeared in numerous comic books, TV shows, films, and video games. He is one of the most popular and commercially successful superheroes, known for his relatable personality and complex character.
